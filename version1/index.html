About

Hi there, my name is Molly and I am a recent graduate from Stanford University studying Computer Science.

I'm in the Human Computer Interaction track, taking fascinating classes from Interaction Design, to iPhone Application Programming, to Needfinding. I am also part of the Stanford Section Leading program where I teach a group of 12 students in the introductory CS classes. I was co-leader of the gender-equality outreach program in CS, organizing bi-quarterly dinners for women considering CS, holding 1:1 chats, and acting as a panalist in conferences. My Senior year I TA'd CS247, the HCI Design Studio! I was very involved in student government, acting as a Resident Assistant in the all- Freshman dorm Trancos. I have kept in touch with the global HCI community as Press Coordinator for the 2013 CHI Conference.

My passion lies in helping design and develop new technology that can help us see the world in new ways. I want to be a part of crafting the future of intuitive software and enabling life-altering developments that can change the way we understand and communicate problems.



I have been passionate about helping build better technology since finding the field of Human Computer Interaction my Freshman year at Stanford. That summer, I interned at Microsoft as a Program Manager learning how to improve organizational efficiency and code reuse. I spent my time at Stanford improving my mobile development skills, improving my knowledge of algorithms in CS161, testing my designer's intuition in Me216A, and learning to lead a group as an RA and Section Leader. I spent my remaining summers as a Google SWE on the Authorship team and as a Google APM in the Zurich office. My Senior year I spent Fall studying abroad in Berlin before finishing up my undergrad with a lot of fun classes in the dschool. I had an amazing experience at Stanford, taking the most interesting classes Stanford has to offer, and now I have embarked on a whole new exciting journey as a full-time APM at Google. Excited to see what the future holds.
